I'm a second-year PhD student of University of California, Los Angeles. My research focuses on robotics.

I earned the Master of Science degree in Mechanical Engineering in 2020 from Columbia University. I was a Research Assistant at the Columbia Robotics Lab advised by Prof. Peter K. Allen. I received a Bachelor of Engineering degree in Vehicle Engineering from Jilin University in 2018.
Publications
Learning Near-global-optimal Combinatorial Strategies for Hybrid Non-convex Model Predictive Control of Single Rigid Body Locomotion
Xuan Lin, Feng Xu, Alexander Schperberg, Dennis Hong
Submitted to Humannoids 2022

In this paper we solve the complete single rigid body optimization problem with a mixed-integer programming (MIP) approach. Offline problems can be solved close to the global optimality.

Real-to-Sim: Deep Learning with Auto-Tuning to Predict Residual Errors using Sparse Data
Alexander Schperberg, Yusuke Tanaka, Feng Xu, Marcel Menner, Dennis Hong
Submitted to Humannoids 2022

In this paper we learn the residual errors between a kinematic and/or simulator model and the real robot with only small amounts of data and demonstrate our method on a wheeled robot and on a two arm manipulator robot.

SQUIRL: Robust and Efficient Learning from Video Demonstration of Long-Horizon Robotic Manipulation Tasks
Bohan Wu, Feng Xu, Zhanpeng He, Abhi Gupta, Peter K. Allen
IROS 2020
paper / website / video

This paper aims to address the commonly-known IRL sample efficiency challenge with a robust, sample-efficient, and general meta-IRL algorithm, SQUIRL, that performs a new but related long-horizon task robustly given only a single video demonstration.

Generative Attention Learning: A "GenerAL" Framework for High-Performance Multi-Fingered Grasping in Clutter
Bohan Wu, Iretiayo Akinola, Abhi Gupta, Feng Xu, Jacob Varley, David Watkins-Valls, Peter K. Allen
Journal of Autonomous Robots (AURO), 2020
paper / website / video

This paper proposes a fully general deep reinforcement learning framework for high-DOF grasping that is not only high performance in dense clutter and novel objects but also effective with robotic hands with arbitrary degrees of freedom.
